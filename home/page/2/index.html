Vibratory Thumbler

Vibratory thumbler is a useful machine, when casting plaster and polishing CNC parts. I use it mainly for bubble-free castings with silicone, epoxy and plaster, as vibrations facilitate the air, trapped in the small pockets near the surface to escape. Old braking disc serves as a solid base, to which spring mounted table is mount. The machine is powered by some old high-pole DC motor, offering low speed and high torque, to which the eccentric cam is mounted for debalance.

Homemade Microoptics

I built several microlens arrays with different technologies, reflow lithography, with subsequent reactive ion etching, electromagnetic imprinting to produce modls, used in an embossing process to produce lenses, and last, ion exchange process to change the refractive index using KNO3 and AGNO3 molten salt baths. I used a lens to build a Shack-Hartmann sensor, which was built entirely from scratch. I was in a need of a microlens array to design and build a wavefront sensor based on Shack-Hartmann principle.

LED Sign

I’ve made this LED display for a friend of mine, who happens to be a big football fan. A stand was carved out of spruce with a 6mm ballnose mill. The stock material has been mount inclined 20° on the CNC while the pocket was curved. The display is made out of plexi glass, engraved with 60° V-cutter, dived 0.5mm in material, with 0.75mm pitch. Underneath the plexiglass, there are 6 green LEDs, secretly placed in the pocket in the stand.

Watch Opener

I have made this wrench to open my brothers watch (Casio Edifice EFR546D) and replace the battery during the Christmas holidays this year. I could just order the watch repair kit from eBay or similair, however lead times were all far longer what we were prepared to wait. We needed it right on spot, so I modelled it in a Fusion360 and went to my workshop. First, I have turned an aluminium rod on the lathe to a size, drilled a bore and cut a M6 thread through its center and parted it off.

Spice Rack

I have made this nice spice rack out of 30mm spruce plank. The spice rack measures 150 x 450 x 30 mm. It was carved in two pieces, as the cnc working area is 300mm in the longest dimension. So I set up two separate gcode, both referenced to the same physical point on the object. The point was found on CNC using a camera, hence As final finish, I used linseed oil, which pops out the grain a bit and gives a nice mate finish.

Blacksmithing

For three years in a row I attended the three days blacksmithing workshop, organized by Slovenian Blacksmith Association, Section Plamen. The workshop took place at the blacksmith shop of the master Jernej Zorko in Župeča Vas, one of the last artistic blacksmiths in Slovenia. Apart of learning basic blacksmithing techniques, we also had the opportunity to try and blacksmith something on our own. I’ve made two sculptures by different technologies. The owl is made by welding steel fathers to a to a smith steel frame.

Deformable Mirror

As a side project of my PhD, I’ve developed an adaptive optical system with a custom built deformable mirror. Deformable mirror was developed out of a piezo buzzer to which at the frontside, a small coated objective glass, ground to the round shape has been bonded. Piezo electrode has been segmented into four electrically isolated segments. By applying different voltages to the segments, the curvature of the mirror can be controlled.

Celtic Love spoon

A wedding gift for a friend Matevž. It is a version of a nordic love-spoon, that once vikings used to make for their loved ones. The hand symbolyse the friendship, the heart the true love, the knot the eternity, and finally the crown the nobleness. The lovespoon is made out of spruce, entirely without power tools, just hacksaw, chisels, sanding paper and elbow grease. Unfortunately, photos of the carving process have been lost through the years.
